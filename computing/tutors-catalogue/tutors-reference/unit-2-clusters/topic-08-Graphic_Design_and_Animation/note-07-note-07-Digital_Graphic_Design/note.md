## Module Aim

This module introduces the student to vector-based illustration software. Students will produce high-quality artwork for both screen and print, and will gain an understanding of the limitations of printing methods for various print media. This module also introduces the student to industry-standard publishing applications for interactive PDF documents, digital magazines, and EPUBs.

---

## Learning Outcomes

On successful completion of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Utilise a range of digital illustration techniques to enable them to develop a multimedia portfolio.
2. Compose digital artwork, using a range of techniques and software, for both digital and print media.
3. Create interactive files, PDF forms and digital publications for various mobile devices.
4. Design and cost a print job for a business or event.

---

## Indicative Content

The module covers the following topics:

- Digital Illustration
- Structuring Media: single and multi-page layout
- Designing for screen vs. designing for print
- Interactive PDFs, Digital Magazines and ePUBs
- Packaging and pre-flight

## Assessment Criteria

### Fail (<40%)
<40%: Unable to interpret and describe key concepts that relate to graphic design.

### Pass (40%-49%)
40%-49%: Be able to interpret and describe key concepts that relate to graphic design.

### Credit (50%-59%)
50%-59%: Ability to discuss key concepts that relate to graphic design and ability to discover and integrate related knowledge in other knowledge domains.

### Distinction (60%-69%)
60%-69%: Be able to solve design problems by experimenting with the appropriate skills and tools.

### High Distinction (70%-100%)
70%-100%: All the above to an excellent level. Be able to analyse and design solutions to a high standard for a range of both complex and unforeseen problems through the use and modification of appropriate skills and tools.
