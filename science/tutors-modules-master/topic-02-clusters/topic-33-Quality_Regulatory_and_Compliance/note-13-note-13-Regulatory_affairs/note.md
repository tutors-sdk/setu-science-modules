## Module Aim

This module will develop the students competency in identifying, interpreting and applying the regulations and procedures involved in obtaining and maintaining marketing authorisations in European and worldwide markets, and procedures to ensure compliance to pharmaceutical legislation.

---

## Learning Outcomes

On successful completion of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Determine the agencies operating within the global pharmaceutical sector and their role in the regulation of all aspects of the regulation for the pharmaceutical industry.
2. Appraise the importance of the role of the regulatory affairs professional and their link to operations functions within the industry from development phase, to ensure compliance at all stages.
3. Compare and contrast the possible application routes to be followed to obtain a marketing authorisation in various markets, and apply a suitable strategy to meet the company's objectives and ensure a successful outcome.
4. Evaluate the relevant information necessary to be submitted to obtain and maintain marketing authorisations in major markets and compilation of the required documentation for submission of initial and ongoing submissions.
5. Monitor the post-marketing obligations of the pharmaceutical manufacturer and MA holder in the on-going safety monitoring of pharmaceutical products to ensure compliance with the regulations.

---

## Indicative Content

The module covers the following topics:

- Introduction to regulatory agencies, national EU and export markets
- Overview of the development of the regulations including directivsd egulations and guidelines including 2001/83/EC
- Role of the regulatory affairs professional in compliance and inspections in the pharmaceutical industry
- Application procedures for Marketing authorisation including National centralised Decentralised nad mutual recognition procedures
- Legal basis for applications, technical requirements and the common technical document
- Maintenance of marketing authorisations , renewals variations
- Introduction to pharmacovigilance

## Assessment Criteria

<40%: No understanding of the basic concept of regulatory affairs and compliance. Unable to carry out critical thinking of subject area.

40%-49%: Is able to understand the regulatory affairs and compliance philosophies. May have some difficulty with critical thinking.

50%-59%: All the above in addition to evaluation of legislation and documentation. Correctly make suggestions for correct incorporation of new legislation and able to write quality documentation.

60%-69%: In addition to the above, good critical thinking and decision making abilities. Shows a high level of competence and efficiency in the subject area.

70%-100%: All previous to excellent level. Be able to describe and discuss regulatory affairs and compliance to the legislation pertaining to the pharmaceutical and healthcare industries.
