## Module Aim

This module examines the impact the food industry has on our environment. The waste management hierarchy is examined in detail as well as an introduction to the challenges facing the food industry. The value of food wastes is examined. The waste management strategy and environmental impact of a food company are investigated as part of a case study. The conversion of food waste into energy is studied through an introduction to a variety of newly established technologies.

---

## Learning Outcomes

On successful completion of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Assess of the causes and effects of pollution from the food industry.
2. Assess the future impact of food security and sustainability.
3. Establish the different food waste types, pre- and post-consumer.
4. Critique the waste management hierarchy model within the circular bioeconomy.
5. Evaluate the importance of greenhouse gas emissions and the carbon footprint from farm to retail food product.
6. Appraise the legislation associated with the management of food wastes.
7. Assess energy conservation and recovery using waste to energy conversion technologies within the food sector.

---

## Indicative Content

The module covers the following topics:

- Effects of food industry pollution on the environment
- Food security and food waste and the circular bioeconomy
- Types of food waste
- Waste management hierarchy model
- Food waste as a resource, food waste to energy technologies
- Greenhouse gases and carbon footprint
- Legislation relating to food waste / processing and the environment
- Energy conservation and recovery within the food sector

## Assessment Criteria

<40%: No understanding of basic principles of environmental aspects of food production and food security.

40%-49%: Able to understand principles; may have some difficulty applying this theory.

50%-59%: Able to understand principles discussed in the course and also able to evaluate practical data and evaluate food processing from an environmental point of view.

60%-69%: In addition to the above, able to demonstrate a high level of competence and efficiency in subject area.

70%-100%: All previous to excellent level. Be able to describe and discuss in detail environmental aspects of food production, processing and food security.
