# Advanced DSP (NUIST)

The aim of this course is to build a deep understanding of DSP through the medium of MATLAB, no prior experience of DSP is assumed.

---

## Module Details

- **Credits:** 5
- **Level:** Postgraduate
- **Department:** Engineering Technology
- **Cluster:** Science and Mathematics

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Use MATLAB to determine the frequency response of a system from its Transfer function.
- Design high pass, low pass and band pass / band stop filters using MATLAB.
- Compute the coefficients and frequency response of filters using MATLAB.
- Develop software to implement Weiner and self tuning adaptive filters.
- Perform analysis using time-frequency representation, multi-level decomposition and basis functions.
- Analyse and design sample rate changing systems and digital filterbanks.

---

## Indicative Content

- Z-Transform and difference equations
- Convolution, correlation and linear predictive coding
- Fourier transforms and filter design, quadrature mirror filters, haar transform
- Wavelet transforms and filter banks
- Wiener filters, Kalman gain and recursive least squres
