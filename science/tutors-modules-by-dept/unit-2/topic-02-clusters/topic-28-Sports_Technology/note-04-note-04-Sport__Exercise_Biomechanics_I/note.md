# Sport & Exe Biomech 1

The aim of this module is to provide students with an introduction to the core elements of sport and exercise biomechanics.

---

## Module Details

- **Credits:** 5
- **Level:** Introductory
- **Department:** Sport and Exercise Science
- **Cluster:** Sports Technology

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Outline the basic biomechanical concepts underpinning movement in sport.
- Perform basic applied calculations relating to kinematic and kinetic quantities (e.g. velocity, acceleration, force).
- Identify the key aspects and applications of equipment used during biomechanical testing.
- Collect biomechanical data of a sports movement (e.g. speed, angular data, jump data).
- Report on collected/sample biomechanical data.

---

## Indicative Content

- Introduction to linear and angular kinematics.
- Introduction to linear and angular kinetics.
- Qualititative biomechanics (e.g. non numerical description of a movement based on direct observation by coaches and teachers).
- Quantitative biomechanics (e.g. numerical measures from data colected during a movement).
- Exemplars of biomechanical skill breakdown
- Introduction to equipment used by sports biomechanists to collect data (e.g. Motion analysis systems, force plates and jump mats, timing gates, and video)
- Fluid dynamics
