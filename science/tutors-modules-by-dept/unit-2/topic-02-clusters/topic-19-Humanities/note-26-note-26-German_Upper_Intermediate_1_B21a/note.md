# German B2.1a

This module aims to enable students to speak, write, listen and read German at level B2.1 of the Common European Framework Reference (CEFR) for Languages.

---

## Module Details

- **Credits:** 5
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Department:** Law and Criminal Justice Studies
- **Cluster:** Humanities

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Speaking: Use a wide range of appropriate language to deal with most everyday situations and deliver short presentations on topics pertinent to everyday occurrences and social interactions.
- Writing: Create both formal and informal texts for work and life based situations.
- Listening: React appropriately to factual information or discussions on work or life based topics.
- Reading: Respond to authentic text from a variety of sources in German.
- Demonstrate Intercultural awareness and independent reflection.

---

## Indicative Content

- Themes that will be used to achieve the learning outcomes for this module will include:
- Personal relationships and family
- The world of work including Health and Safety
- New media
- Various cultural themes including portraits of cities in German speaking countries
- Linguistic structures will build on those introduced in Level B1.2. Additional forms to be covered in this module include:
- Conjunctions: coordinating; subordinating; causing inversion; two part
- Syntactic features of main clauses
- Passive to express a state or process
- Reference pronouns
