# German B2.1b

This module aims to enable students to speak, write, listen and read German to level B2.1 of the Common European Framework Reference (CEFR) for Languages.

---

## Module Details

- **Credits:** 5
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Department:** Law and Criminal Justice Studies
- **Cluster:** Humanities

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Speaking: Debate topical issues while displaying a good lexical range in an appropriate register.
- Writing: Produce creative, documentative or emotive texts in various genres specific to the context.
- Listening: React appropriately to and identify the different components of complex speech on varied topics.
- Reading: Display both skimming and scanning techniques in reading as well as the ability to infer the meaning of unknown, non-culturally specific, lexical items from context.
- Demonstrate intercultural awareness and independent reflection.

---

## Indicative Content

- Themes that will be used to achieve the learning outcomes for this module will include:
- Studying and living abroad
- Health, fitness and personal image
- Politics
- Various cultural themes including portraits of cities in German speaking countries
- Environment
- Linguistic structures will build on those introduced in Level B1.2. Additional forms to be covered in this module include:
- Word formation
- Future perfect
- Nominalising of verbs
- Adjectives with prepositions
- Conditional tense for wishes, conditions and comparisons
