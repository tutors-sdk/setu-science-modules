# German B1.1

This module aims to enable students to speak, write, listen and read German to level B1.1 of the Common European Framework Reference(CEFR) for Languages.

---

## Module Details

- **Credits:** 5
- **Level:** Introductory
- **Department:** Law and Criminal Justice Studies
- **Cluster:** Humanities

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Speaking: Maintain spontaneous conversations by exchanging information and expressing opinions on familiar topics. Give a prepared presentation which is articulated clearly.
- Writing: Write more complex accounts of experiences as well as personal texts.
- Listening: Display evidence of understanding of general points and some specific details about common topics and related to the content of the module. Identify the main points in media clips.
- Reading: Identify the main themes and some detail in written texts on familiar and unfamiliar topics in known genres. Deduce meaning of unknown words and phrases from context and familiarity with the topic.
- Demonstrate intercultural awareness based on class discussion and independent reflection.

---

## Indicative Content

- Themes that will be used to achieve the learning outcomes for this module will include:
- Biographies of interesting people
- Book and film critique
- Consumer Behaviour and Advertising
- Describing statistics and graphs
- Various cultural themes including employment and media in German speaking countries
- Linguistic structures will build on those introduced in Level A2.2/Leaving Certificate German. Additional forms to be covered in this module include:
- Grammatical structures using past tenses with regular and irregular verbs. Prepositonal verbs and Adjective agreements
- Subordinate clauses, infinitive sentences
