# French Intermediate 1 (B1.1)

To facilitate students' language learning to meet an Intermediate level commensurate with CEFR B1.1.

---

## Module Details

- **Credits:** 5
- **Level:** Introductory
- **Department:** Law and Criminal Justice Studies
- **Cluster:** Humanities

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Speaking: Converse spontaneously by exchanging information and expressing opinions on familiar topics through clear articulation.
- Writing: Write more complex accounts of experiences as well as personal texts.
- Listening: Display evidence of understanding of general points and some specific details about common and topics related to the content of the module. Identify the main points in media clips which relate to the content.
- Reading: Identify the main themes and some detail in written texts on familiar and unfamiliar topics in known genres. Deduce meaning of unknown words and phrases from context and familiarity with the topic.

---

## Indicative Content

- A compendium of language exercises and tasks from the prescribed textbook Savoir-faire - Le français a' l'universite'.
- Contexts / themes that may be used to achieve the learning outcomes for this level may include the following:
- Curriculum vitae
- Psychological description of people
- France in French cinema
- Education and college education
- Conditional present, forming hypothesis/es
- Reflexive verbs (present)
- Direct and indirect personal pronouns
- Expression of the past: perfect and imperfect
- Comphrehension using literary extracts
- Parlons français, c'est facile (web docs)
- Feminine adjectives
