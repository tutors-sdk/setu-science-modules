# Protein Chemistry & Enzymology

This module will provide students with a detailed description of protein and enzyme structure and function and also examines protein/enzyme purification and analysis.

---

## Module Details

- **Credits:** 5
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Department:** Science
- **Cluster:** Biology

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Communicate the various levels of protein structure.
- Examine a variety of extraction and purification methods used for proteins/enzymes.
- Explore the catalytic principles and the biochemistry of enzyme action.
- Compare and contrast different types of enzyme inhibition and determine the importance of enzyme inhibitors in pharmacology and biochemistry.
- Analyse and interpret data obtained from experimental analysis of proteins and enzymes and communicate these effectively.

---

## Indicative Content

- Primary, secondary, tertiary and quaternary structure of proteins
- Denaturation and precipitation of proteins
- Protein and enzyme extraction and purification methods
- Principles of enzyme catalysis: substrate specificity, chemical nature of enzyme catalysis, cofactors
- Enzyme inhibition and clinical inhibitors
- Enzyme technology: industrial applications, immobilisation and biosensors
